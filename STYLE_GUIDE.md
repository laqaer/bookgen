# STYLE GUIDE

## Voice

- Narrative nonfiction with institutional rigor.
- Analytical, clear, and sober.
- Confident in method, cautious in claims.

## Tone

- High-signal, low-drama.
- No sensational framing.
- Emphasize documented process over personality speculation.

## Default Language Rules

- Prefer: "suggests", "supports", "documents", "contested", "evidence indicates".
- Avoid absolute certainty unless directly documented.
- Distinguish observed facts, inferences, and open questions.

## Taboo List

- Do not claim to prove a unified hidden cabal.
- Do not use defamatory assertions about named individuals.
- Do not fabricate quotes, pages, or direct-source wording.
- Do not present uncertainty as proof of conspiracy.
- Do not use rhetorical escalation to replace evidence.

## Recurring Motifs

- Cave motif: limited, disciplined use to represent structured visibility and epistemic limits.
- Ledger motif: who has the full record versus summary access.
- Architecture motif: secrecy as design, not mystical force.

## Chapter Structure Standard

- `#` title
- Epigraph line (original prose)
- `## Orientation`
- `## Core Claim` with 3-6 bullets
- `## Institutional Mechanics`
- `## Case Studies` with 2-4 subsections where applicable
- `## Analytical Synthesis`
- `## What This Changes`
- `## Skeptic's Corner` with a steelman objection

## Citation Policy

- Use placeholder syntax: `[CITATION: short note]`.
- No fabricated links or quotations.
- Prefer primary institutional records where possible.
- Mark contested interpretations explicitly.

## Continuity Rules

- Preserve consistent definitions across chapters.
- Reintroduce key concepts briefly when reused.
- Keep skepticism method-centered, not personality-centered.
