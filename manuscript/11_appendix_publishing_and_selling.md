# Appendix: Publishing and Selling Plan

*Epigraph:* Publishing discipline is part of argument discipline.

## Purpose

This appendix gives a practical route from draft to market without sacrificing credibility. The emphasis is operational: sequencing, quality control, and risk management.

## 1. Editorial Pipeline (Step by Step)

1. Developmental edit pass: test argument architecture, chapter order, repeated claims, and unsupported leaps.
2. Evidence pass: replace each placeholder with a source plan, mark unresolved assertions, and downgrade overconfident language.
3. Line edit pass: tighten paragraphs, remove rhetorical drift, and enforce style guide consistency.
4. Fact-check pass: validate names, dates, institutional terminology, and legal framing.
5. Legal sensitivity pass: review potentially defamatory or overbroad statements, especially where motive is inferred.
6. Proofread pass: fix punctuation, continuity, and front/back matter consistency.

## 2. Self-Publishing Workflow (KDP + IngramSpark)

1. Prepare two production files: print interior PDF and reflowable EPUB.
2. Build separate trim-size templates for paperback and hardback where relevant.
3. Upload to KDP first for Amazon ecosystem alignment and quick proof cycles.
4. Upload to IngramSpark for broader print distribution and bookstore ordering pathways.
5. Order physical proofs from both platforms and compare margin, grayscale rendering, and spine alignment.
6. Lock metadata only after proof corrections are complete.

## 3. Audiobook Workflow (ACX)

1. Create a narration script pass that removes citation brackets from spoken text and clarifies acronyms.
2. Decide production model: DIY narrator, royalty share, or pay-for-production.
3. Produce a 15-minute sample across exposition and case-study sections to test tone.
4. Standardize pronunciation notes for institutional terms and names.
5. Conduct QC on pacing, room tone, and chapter transition consistency.
6. Publish through ACX and align launch window with print and ebook campaigns.

## 4. Cover and Interior Design

1. Cover concept brief: institutional gravity, restraint, and readability at thumbnail size.
2. Test title hierarchy with subtitle legibility in mobile storefront previews.
3. Interior design: choose one serif for body text and one sans for headings; avoid decorative overload.
4. Add pull-quote style only if quotes are verified and non-defamatory.
5. Validate print contrast for charts or diagrams before final export.

## 5. Launch Plan (Email List + Podcasts + Substack)

1. Pre-launch window (6-8 weeks): publish excerpt essays and a methodological note on evidence standards.
2. Build an email sequence: problem framing, chapter excerpts, launch announcement, and post-launch resources.
3. Podcast outreach: prioritize shows focused on governance, media, public administration, and civic literacy.
4. Substack strategy: serialize short sections that demonstrate rigor without sensational framing.
5. Launch week: coordinate newsletter, podcast appearances, and social excerpts tied to one central landing page.
6. Post-launch month: collect reader objections, publish clarifications, and reinforce credibility through transparent corrections.

## 6. Pricing, Categories, and Keywords (General Guidance)

1. Price print for sustainable unit economics after distribution cuts and proofing costs.
2. Price ebook to encourage initial adoption while preserving perceived seriousness.
3. Select categories where governance and political institutions readers actually browse.
4. Use keywords focused on institutional analysis, secrecy policy, oversight, and democratic accountability.
5. Revisit metadata after the first 60-90 days based on conversion data and reader feedback.

## 7. Credibility Checklist (Pre-Publication)

- Every high-impact claim has a source plan or is explicitly marked as contested.
- No sentence implies a unified hidden cabal without documentary evidence.
- Speculative language is tagged with careful qualifiers.
- Case studies distinguish known facts from interpretation.
- Counterarguments are steelmanned in each chapter.
- Legal review completed for potentially sensitive passages.
- Final manuscript tone remains sober, high-signal, and non-sensational.

## What This Changes

Publishing quality becomes part of epistemic quality. A careful release process does not slow the argument; it protects the argument from preventable credibility loss.

## Skeptic's Corner

A critic may say operational publishing guidance is outside the intellectual core of the book. The counterpoint is practical: claims about public knowledge fail in practice when production errors, weak sourcing discipline, or sloppy metadata prevent serious readers from trusting the work.
