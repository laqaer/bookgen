# Market Analysis

## Market Problem

Readers interested in power and secrecy face two poor options: technical literature that is inaccessible to broad audiences, or sensational narratives that overclaim.

## Market Opportunity

A disciplined, narrative nonfiction title can serve readers who want analytic clarity without conspiratorial framing. This segment includes policy-curious professionals, journalists, graduate students, and civically engaged readers.

## Demand Drivers

- Persistent public trust volatility around institutions.
- High attention to information quality and media credibility.
- Growth of long-form newsletter and podcast ecosystems.

## Category Fit

- Politics and current affairs (institutional analysis)
- Public policy and governance
- Media literacy and democratic accountability

## Risks

- Polarization can distort reception.
- Nuanced framing may be less viral than sensational framing.

## Mitigations

- Strong excerpt strategy with clear, quotable claims.
- Consistent boundary language in all marketing assets.
- Structured launch campaign with credibility-first messaging.
